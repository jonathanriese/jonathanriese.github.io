Le répit du travailleur – Jeanne Menjoulet

Le répit du travailleur (Respite of the Worker)

The statue shows a naked, muscular man sitting on a rock with his head resting on his two hands on a pickaxe, which has, however, disappeared. Carved from white marble, it was made by Jean Jules Pendariès (1862-1933) in 1907 to be exhibited at the Salon, and placed in its current location, the Esplanade de Roger Linet, in 1926. It is located in front of the Maison des Métallos, the House of Steelworkers, which was owned by the metal worker’s union.
